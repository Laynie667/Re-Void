-- STEP 8: Handle interactions --

roomzone handle eevee/fur = You pick up the Eevee. It is lighter than a properly stuffed piece of this size should be — the fill has migrated over years, concentrated at the head and tail, thinner through the body where hands grip. The fur yields under your palm in grain lines laid down by thousands of passes in the same direction. It smells faintly of the bed, the room, of repeated use. You are briefly, clearly aware of handling something that belongs here in a specific way — something that has been held often, and returned to the same position each time. You set it back in the center of the bed, facing the door.

roomzone handle eevee/collar = You work the tiny violet collar out from where it is half-tucked in the ruff, using your fingertips — it is small enough that anything less precise catches. The snap is smooth from many openings. You turn the tag to find the reverse. |mA.R.|n — two letters and a period, smaller than the name on the nest's collar, done by the same hand. You fasten the collar back and tuck it into position.

roomzone handle nest/chains = You step into the nest and sit in the hollow at its center, then reach for the chains. They pay out with a soft chain-sound, each link catching and releasing in sequence. The cuffs fit correctly — the padding dense enough that the metal edge is imperceptible when closed. Holding them over your wrists without fastening, you are aware of how precisely the length has been set. There is exactly enough chain to be comfortable in the deepest part of the nest, and nothing beyond that. You set the chains aside and stand.

roomzone handle nest/collar = The steel is cool when you lift it — not dramatically so, just the ambient cool that mass holds. You press the padded inner face against your throat without closing the clasp. The padding is exactly the right density: present enough to feel, soft enough to be only that. The clasp requires two deliberate motions: safety latch, then the collar. You close it again in reverse and let it hang between the chains.

roomzone handle bookcase/wild_things = You pull |wWhere the Wild Things Submit|n from the shelf. It comes out easily — the gap already maintained. The cover shows a small figure in a paper crown, arms spread, surrounded by creatures twice its height who look down with an expression that is not quite predatory and not quite benevolent. The spine crease opens without resistance to a page a third through, where the book has been read most often. You read the passage. Halfway through, you become aware that the bookcase is not flush against the wall — a narrow gap at the left edge where the stone shows behind it. You push gently. The bookcase gives inward, smoothly, on a hinge that has been maintained.

roomzone handle wardrobe/trunk = You open the trunk's latches — both snap cleanly — and lift the lid. The resistance gives to a smell: the same fabric softener as the rest of the room, with the accumulated warmth of soft things compressed together. The trunk is full of clothing: cottons, fleeces, a few silks, most casual and comfortable. Some pieces are sized smaller than standard adult — proportioned for someone between sizes, or for adult play. You close the lid carefully and re-latch both closures.
