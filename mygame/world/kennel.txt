roomzone detail blanket/wetness = The dampness in the blanket is not uniform. roomzone detail blanket/wear = The blanket's edges show a specific kind of wear — not the generalized pilling of age but the thinning that comes from being held and twisted, the same sections gripped repeatedly. roomzone handle floor/drawings = You crouch and pick up the nearest drawing. The paper is slightly soft at the edges from the kennel's close air. The crayon lines are firm — pressed hard, the way someone presses when they mean it. You look at the three figures in the snow and have the particular sensation of handling something that was never meant to be found by anyone who wasn't already expected.

roomzone handle floor/crayon_fill = You run a finger along one of the crayon-filled grouge lines. The wax is smooth at the surface and gives slightly under pressure — it was packed in warm, worked with fingertips, each color pressed in separately. The claw marks that run beneath the wax are deep enough that you can feel them through the fill, the original shape of the damage still legible under the reclamation.

roomzone handle bars/drawings_taped = You touch the edge of one of the taped drawings and it holds. The gum that fixed it has dried and bonded to both surfaces over time. Pulling too hard would tear the paper rather than free it. You do not pull too hard. You smooth it back against the bar and it settles into place the way it has settled a hundred times before.

roomzone handle blanket/wear = You pick up the blanket. It is lighter than it should be for its size — worn thin at the center, the fleece thinned to near-nothing at the grip corners. It holds the warmth of the kennel floor rather than resisting it. Holding the worn corner, you can feel where the thread count has dropped to almost nothing, the fabric between your fingers a memory of itself. You set it back exactly as it was.

roomzone handle eevee/construction = You pick up the Eevee. It is lighter than a properly stuffed toy should be — the fill has shifted and compressed over years of use. The handmade seams are rougher than they look from a distance, the thread pulled tight in some places and loose in others. It smells like the blanket corner: the specific, private scent of this space, worked into the fabric through years of being held. The |mviolet-striped panties|n shift slightly on its head when you move it. You hold the Eevee for a moment, aware of the count marks on the bar beside you, aware of the drawings on the floor, aware of the kennel door at the foot of the bed above. Then you set it back down, facing the door.
