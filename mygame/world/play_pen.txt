From this side, the arch is the only familiar-proportioned thing in view. Stepping back through it would restore you. You are aware of this without it feeling like urgency.

roomzone desc forest = The |wforest biome|n occupies the near left quarter of the pen — foam trees with actual branching canopy structure, the ground between them covered in low foam undergrowth that shifts when stepped through. roomzone desc mountain = The |wfrost mountain zone|n dominates the back half of the pen, its peaks rising to twice your current height, the foam stone textured and cold when touched. The sand between the dunes is real sand, the fine powder that coats anything that passes through it. Tracks in it from previous visits are still partially preserved.

roomzone desc figurines = The figurines throughout the pen are not static. Near your current position, an |morc figurine|n with a heavy build and an expression that contains the word |xgrunts|n in it is looking at you with the specific attentiveness of something that has noticed you and assessed you and arrived at a conclusion it hasn't shared yet.

roomzone detail arch/threshold = The threshold of the arch, examined at your current scale, has a worn path through the wicker base — the kind that forms when something passes repeatedly. From this side, passing through would mean stepping into a room that is very large, or resuming being someone who fits it. Both descriptions are accurate. The choice appears simple.

roomzone detail arch/inscription = Woven into the wicker at eye height on the interior arch: text in a script that is not quite any alphabet you know but that resolves, when you hold your attention on it, into something close to: |mYou are the right size now.|n Whether this is encouragement or description is unclear.

roomzone detail forest/tiger = The tiger figurine nearest the path into the forest biome is watching you. It has the density of real muscle at this scale — its shoulders are at your shoulder height, the mass of the body behind them substantial. Its expression is the particular expression of a predator that is not hungry and does not need to be. Its anatomical features are at full scale, which at this size means something specific. It makes a sound: not a roar, something lower. Conversational.

roomzone detail forest/elf = The elf figurines in the forest have the quality of things built to observe. They occupy the trees — seated on branches, standing at trunks — and they watch the activity below with the relaxed attention of something that has seen this before and found it worthwhile. One, sitting at eye level in a branch above the path, has an expression that could be described as welcoming. It is dressed in leaves and not much else. It is looking at you.

roomzone detail mountain/yeti = The yeti figurines on the mountain's lower slopes are enormous at your scale — a head and shoulders above you, their white fur actually warm when you press a hand into it, the material giving like real fur rather than foam. One near the mountain path stops moving when you approach and regards you. Its expression is patient. Its anatomy is as specifically rendered as everything else in the pen, the scale of it at this proximity impossible to mistake.

roomzone detail mountain/dragon = One of the snow dragons is coiled at the mountain's mid-slope, its scale structure visible at close range — each scale individually formed, the color graduating from slate at the back to almost white at the underbelly. It is asleep, or doing a complete impression of asleep. Its breathing moves its sides. The underside anatomy, visible from where it rests, is at the same scale of careful production as every other inhabitant of this pen.

roomzone detail desert/sand = The desert sand is real fine powder and it adheres. Walking through the dunes coats your feet and lower legs. The tracks already present in the sand are of several different shapes — some human-proportioned, some not. Several sets of tracks converge on one point in the center of the largest dune cluster and do not diverge from it. Something happened there and the sand still holds the evidence.

roomzone detail figurines/orc = The |morc figurine|n who has been watching you since you oriented has not moved, but it has registered your attention. Up close: it is a head taller than you, heavy-shouldered, the figurine construction detailed in the way of something that was built to specific requirements. Its expression has the quality of someone who has made a decision and is waiting for the appropriate moment to share it with you. It makes a sound. It is not a question.

-- STEP 8: Handle interactions --

roomzone handle arch/threshold = You put your hand through the arch threshold. On the other side, your hand is full-sized again — normal scale, normal weight, the cuff of your sleeve suddenly enormous against the wicker frame from this side. You pull it back. The transition is immediate and seamless, your hand returning to current dimensions as it crosses the plane back. The arch holds this open without apparent mechanism: just the threshold, and the choice.

roomzone handle forest/tiger = You step toward the tiger. It doesn't retreat. Up close, the fur is not foam — the texture under your hand is something warmer, denser, the specific give of something that breathes. It makes the low sound again, closer now, the vibration of it present in your chest as much as heard. It is not threatening you. It is waiting. What it is waiting for is legible.

roomzone handle mountain/yeti = You put your hand into the yeti's fur and it turns toward you — not quickly, not startled, just orienting. The fur is warm. The mass behind it is real. It looks down at you with the patient expression it had when you were farther away, and at this proximity its anatomical features are at a scale that has implications for you personally. It doesn't move toward you. It is giving you the decision.

roomzone handle figurines/orc = The orc figurine stops waiting when you approach and reaches for you — not a grab, more the confident taking-hold of something that has been offered. Its grip is full-handed and certain, and it exerts not force but direction: downward. It makes the sound again. Closer, it has more texture — low, resonant, the kind of sound that comes before words in a language that doesn't need them. You are on your knees before you registered choosing to be. The figurine's other hand comes to rest on the back of your head. It is in no hurry.
