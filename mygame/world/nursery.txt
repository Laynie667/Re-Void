roomzone detail crib/bars = The crib bars are solid steel finished in white, the paint still fully intact at the tops where they're not touched but worn through to bare metal at the lower two-thirds where hands grip them from inside. The wear is not from assembly or storage. The bar spacing is consistent with comfort rather than maximum restriction: enough to feel present, not enough to feel punishing.

roomzone detail crib/blankets = The blankets are flannel and fleece, layered in alternating textures — soft against skin regardless of which way a body is turned. The topmost blanket has a pattern of small moons and stars on a pale yellow ground. The stuffed wolf in the red ribbon has been placed at the head end, positioned upright against the inner bars, both eyes facing into the crib's center. It has been placed there consistently enough that the blanket beneath it shows the compression.

roomzone detail crib/stuffed_animals = Beyond the wolf: a round brown bear whose ear seam has been repaired twice (the stitching is in two different thread colors at the same point), and a rabbit whose ribbon has been tied and retied many times, the ribbon fabric fraying at the knot area. The wolf is the newest addition — its ribbon is still crisp.

roomzone detail window/wolf_tracks = Stepping close to the glass and looking out, the wolf tracks are clearer. They enter the visible area of the yard from the right edge — where the treeline starts — and exit to the left edge without veering. The stride length is long. The tracks are fresh enough that the pressed snow still holds the toe impression clearly. There is no corresponding track in the other direction. Whoever walked there walked in one direction and did not come back this way.

roomzone detail window/seat = The window seat cushions compress unevenly from use — deepest at the center-left, where a body sits and leans toward the glass. The outermost cushion has a small hand-sized wear mark at its edge where the fabric has taken a grip repeatedly. From this seat, the wolf tracks are directly in the sightline. You have the distinct impression someone has sat here and watched the forest for a long time on multiple occasions.

roomzone detail changing_table/diapers = The diaper stack is a thick, crinkle-heavy type — the kind with extra padding built in, the sort that can't be hidden under most clothing and is not designed to be. The top several show evidence of handling: the topmost is slightly offset from the stack, suggesting it has been picked up and set back at least once. The size printed on the packaging is not the smallest available, and not the largest.

roomzone detail changing_table/teddy = The teddy bear propped against the table leg is large — the lap-sized kind rather than a shelf display. It is positioned upright, both arms resting on its thighs, its expression the standard flat-stitched smile of factory production but with a softness in the face from age that gives it a quality the factory didn't intend. It smells faintly of the room's powdery scent.

roomzone detail nursing_chair/arm_wear = The worn patch on the chair's left arm is palm-width and at a height consistent with someone resting their arm while holding something against the right side of their chest. The fabric is soft there in a way it is not anywhere else — a genuine softness from compression, not wear. Sitting in the chair and resting your arm in this position, you find the height is exactly right without adjustment.

roomzone detail shelves/colouring = The piece of colouring on the wall was done in crayon and marker. The pond is a careful blue oval, the trees rendered in the way children render trees — lollipop shapes, pressed hard enough that the crayon transferred fully. The plantlife is unusually specific: the greens are layered in two or three colors each, and the purples alongside them are a particular shade that was clearly chosen deliberately rather than grabbed at random. The dirt path leading away from the water disappears at the edge of the paper. Someone pressed very hard on the brown crayon to make the path feel solid.

-- STEP 8: Handle interactions --

roomzone handle crib/bars = You put both hands on the crib bars and lean in. The structure doesn't flex — solid at the frame, solid at the floor mounts. The bars are cold at the top where they're untouched, and warmer than the rest of the room at the mid-height where the paint has been worn away from handling. You lower the side rail and step in, and the mattress takes your weight without sound. The blankets are exactly as warm as they look. The wolf plushie regards you from the pillow end without moving. From inside, the crib is larger than it appears from outside — the dimensions built for an adult in a specific state, not a child. You lie back, and the blankets settle around you.

roomzone handle window/seat = You settle into the window seat and lean against the glass. The cold transfers through at once — pleasant rather than uncomfortable, a clean edge against your shoulder. The wolf tracks in the snow are directly ahead of you. From this position, at this distance, you can see that the stride is long and deliberate — not the irregular pattern of a dog or a domestic animal but something that knows exactly where it is going. The seat gives under you with the particular softness of something used in this exact way many times before you.

roomzone handle changing_table/diapers = You pick up the top diaper from the stack and unfold it. The crinkling is immediate and specific — the sound designed to be discreet in the way that draws attention to itself by trying not to. The padding is thick, the tapes are wide, and the interior is lined with a material meant to be comfortable for extended wear. You refold it carefully and return it to the stack.

roomzone handle nursing_chair/arm_wear = You sit in the nursing chair and rest your left arm where it has been rested before. The worn patch receives your arm at exactly the right height. The chair begins to glide — the mechanism so smooth it starts without intention, just the slight shift of weight. Sitting here, holding the right side of your chest as if there were something there, facing the window and the wolf tracks: you understand what this chair has been used for.
