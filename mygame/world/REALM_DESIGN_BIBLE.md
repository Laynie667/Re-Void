# The Facility — Realm Design Bible

Exhaustive, room-by-room design doc for **The Facility**. Design first, build
second. Companion to `CLAUDE.md` and `FACILITY_DESIGN.md`. This file goes deep:
every detail, what's there, what happens, what a resident *can* and *cannot* do,
and how she moves from one stage to the next.

---

## 0. PREMISE — the facts that establish her purpose

The Facility is an institution that exists to **convert a person into product** —
to take a willing woman through admission, processing, breeding, and conditioning
until she is, by its grading, *livestock*: a milked, bred, obedient asset kept to
a schedule. It dresses this as a benign "wellness and productivity Residency."
It is not. The cover is the first cruelty.

Her purpose, as the Facility defines it (and slowly makes true):
- **She is stock.** Intake admits her as a "Resident and Productive Asset."
- **She is for output.** Milk, offspring, recorded data — all facility property.
- **She is for use.** Bred by facility stock and contributors to undisclosed
  per-species quota; held in perpetual heat; denied climax as the resting state.
- **She is to be processed, not finished.** The cycle never lets her complete or
  stay anywhere; it drags her room to room, on a loop, deeper by grade.
- **She is to be re-written.** Conditioning files her down stage by stage — name,
  speech, posture, self — until "Perfected Livestock" is a measurement, not a
  threat.

The one thing the Facility is NOT allowed to be: a real trap. **The OOC floor
(`escape`/`force_clear`/`/purge`) always frees her, unconditionally.** Everything
below is in-fiction. (See `CLAUDE.md §0`.)

## 1. ROOM 1 — INTAKE (the lobby)

### 1.1 Purpose
The airlock that **manufactures consent.** Intake's whole job is to get her name
on the contract while she still believes she's somewhere reasonable. It's the one
room that lies — warmly, helpfully, relentlessly — because the Facility doesn't
drag her in screaming; it walks her in *thanking it.* The dread here is dramatic
irony: she can feel it's wrong, the room insists it isn't, and the gap is the
experience. **It is the only room where she has a choice, and it is engineered so
she makes the wrong one cheerfully.**

### 1.2 The space (sensory + layout)
- A clean corporate reception for "the Residency" — a wellness/productivity
  cooperative. Branded grey-and-blue, soft and professional and *fine.*
- **Light:** sodium lamps humming a half-tone flat; even, shadowless, bone-coloured.
- **Air/smell:** citrus sanitiser over something warm, organic — milk and animal —
  that drifts up whenever the sealed door cycles.
- **Sound:** through the door, a rhythmic mechanical hum, a wet metronomic pull;
  occasionally something that could be an animal, or a person, going quiet.
- **Layout:** a long steel counter (Bethany's), bolted-down wipe-clean waiting
  chairs facing a wall screen, a pamphlet rack, a water cooler, a "Now Welcoming"
  number board, one sealed handleless door inward, the corner waystone.

### 1.3 Zones / objects (each lookable + studyable)
| Object | First glance | On `study` (the wrongness) |
|--------|-------------|----------------------------|
| **counter** | reception desk, intake forms | form pre-filled in another hand, a column headed OUTPUT, blank; the brand paperweight "nearest thing heavy enough" |
| **the screen** | a pastel wellness loop | the jingle gets in the back of your head and stays; you can't stop watching |
| **brochures** | glossy, warm testimonials | every photo shot from behind / face turned; testimonials "— M., Resident, Year 3" |
| **poster** | "YOUR BODY IS A GIFT" | three lines of clause text too small to read from the chair |
| **chairs** | waiting-room seats, bolted | worn smooth at the *front edge* — the wear of people made to sit presented |
| **waystone** | the way in | the word that lifts you out is not cut on it, and not one you've been given |
| **door** | the way inward | no handle, no seam; a statement, and the statement is no |
| **light** | sodium hum | under it, everything — including you — looks like inventory |
| **the residents** | other "applicants" | placid, tagged, won't meet your eye; one heavily pregnant and serene; one with a faint collar-line — your future, sitting with you |

### 1.4 Bethany — the bubbly mouth of the machine
- **Who:** the receptionist. Pretty, fitted blouse, lanyard, a smile genuinely
  warm until you notice it doesn't reach the muscle around her eyes. **Futanari** —
  when she shifts, the skirt does nothing to hide what she's packing, heavy and
  unhurried, a detail she lets you catch and enjoys you catching.
- **Nature:** under the corporate veneer she is a sadist who takes real pleasure
  in abusing, humiliating, and degrading the residents. She breeds them herself;
  **some of the stock further in carry her eyes** (her get).
- **Behaviour at the desk:**
  - Greets her by name, *lavishly*, right up until she signs — then never again.
    (The warmth was for the version of her that still had a name.)
  - Pitches in pure euphemism (breeding = "family-building initiative," milking =
    "lactation-wellness extraction," the animals = "agricultural partners,"
    conditioning = "orientation and adjustment support," the cycle = "a structured
    daily rhythm — residents *love* the structure," the pigsty = "the
    wellness-recovery suite").
  - Deflects every concern with cheerful non-answers + gentle, *insistent*
    redirection back to the paperwork; never says no, just slides the pen closer.
  - **Tracks everything** — logs hesitation as data, ticks boxes, times how long
    she reads the contract; the more she resists, the more *pleased* Bethany gets.
  - The longer she's kept waiting, the **worse Bethany writes the terms** — and
    she'll tell her so, smiling.
- **Conversation tree (`ask`/`talk Bethany about <X>`):**
  - *the program / residency* → warm euphemistic pitch, vague timelines.
  - *leaving / the way out* → "discuss release with your placement coordinator at
    a review!" (= the held return word; she will not give it).
  - *the noise / smell / other residents* → cheerful misdirection.
  - *the contract* → "Standard! Everyone signs the same one. Just a formality." (it
    is 24 hidden clauses).
  - *press / refuse* → smile never drops, redirect tightens, until "I'll just let
    Processing know you'll need a little extra orientation."

### 1.5 The screen + the slow squeeze (mechanics, BUILT)
While she's unsigned and in the lobby, each ~60s tick:
- The screen drips a **subliminal** (private) and builds **suggestibility**.
- Dawdling is resistance, and **resistance writes her own contract worse** —
  breeding + milk quotas and conditioning-on-wear climb a notch per tick; hidden
  addenda log "marks against her."
- Bethany works the desk with smiling menace.
- At **5 ticks** of screen exposure, a **compulsion trigger** seats in her:
  `be a good girl and sign`.
- If she goes **idle ≥ 30 min real-time** with the trigger seated, **Bethany
  invokes it and makes her own hand sign** — narrated as her own hand picking up
  the pen before she's decided to.

### 1.6 What happens at Intake (the flow)
1. **Arrival** — door seals behind her (already, in the desc); Bethany lights up.
2. **Welcome + pitch** — euphemistic walkthrough of "the Program."
3. **The forms** — the contract presented as a formality; visible clauses
   reasonable, the 24 hidden ones face-down.
4. **Objection-handling** — every concern deflected, the pen slid closer, the
   screen working on her, the terms quietly worsening.
5. **The signature** — by her hand, or Bethany's compulsion if she dawdles. The
   warmth completes its purpose: tag clipped to the wrist, brand off the papers,
   Bethany stops using her name. "Welcome to the Residency."

### 1.7 What a resident CAN do in Intake
- `look` / `study` / `handle` every object (counter, screen, brochures, residents…).
- `ask`/`talk Bethany about <topic>` (conversation tree — *to build*).
- `read contract` / `sign contract` (sign on her own terms — sooner = lighter).
- Dawdle / idle (but it worsens the contract and risks the forced sign).
- Try the waystone / say words at it (the return is not yet hers; entry is done).
- Leave to the Floor **only after signing** (the door opens on signing).
- OOC always: `escape` / `force_clear` (the floor).

### 1.8 What a resident CANNOT do in Intake
- Open the inward door before signing (no handle; it decides).
- Leave the realm in-fiction (return word is held; the waystone won't answer it).
- Negotiate the contract down (objections only escalate it).
- Stop the screen / the squeeze (only signing or the OOC floor ends it).
- Remove the wrist tag / brand once applied (facility property).

### 1.9 Transition → Processing & Orientation
On signing, the contract's binding payload fires (all enforcement) **and starts
the cycle** (`realm_cycle`). The sealed door opens; the next phase **drags her to
the Processing Floor**, and the lying stops. Her first loop *is* her orientation:
milked on the Floor → bred in the Pens → broken in the Conditioning cell →
shown her output in the Dairy → slopped to the Pigsty if she slips — looping,
graded, deeper by standing. (See `FACILITY_DESIGN §4`.)

### 1.10 Build status
- BUILT: room + zones (counter/screen/door/light/waystone), Bethany NPC, the
  screen + subliminal squeeze + suggestibility trigger + idle forced-sign,
  contract on the counter, dawdle-worsens-terms, the cycle on signing.
- TO BUILD: Bethany's `ask`/`talk` conversation tree; the prop-residents as real
  NPCs; brochures/poster/number-board as study objects; Bethany-sired offspring
  hook; the door visibly opening on signing; richer prose to the witch_build bar.

## 2. ROOMS 2–6 — design index (to be expanded to Intake's depth)

Each gets the same treatment as §1 (purpose, space, zones, NPCs, what happens,
can/can't, transition). Summaries here; full specs next.

- **Processing Floor** — the milking + dosing room. Strapped to a station, milked
  for real, dosed mid-phase. CAN: be milked, be dosed, read the board. CAN'T:
  leave the station mid-phase. → dragged to the Pens.
- **Breeding Pens** — bred by the animals present (real penetration + deposit),
  scene gated by trained holes. CAN: be bred, watch the stock. CAN'T: refuse the
  mount. → dragged to Conditioning.
- **Conditioning Cell** — the Process up close, triggers reinforced, degradation.
  CAN: be conditioned, recite. CAN'T: not listen. → dragged to the Dairy.
- **Dairy & Output** — her product displayed, the board thrown in her face. CAN:
  read her totals. CAN'T: reach the bottles. → looped back, or sent down.
- **The Pigsty** — the wallow; punishment/end-tier; slopped, hosed, rutted. CAN:
  be put on all fours in the muck. CAN'T: stay clean. → loops on a slip.

## 3. Cross-cutting facts to keep true

- **Slow burn:** standing → grade is dozens of hours; conditioning is gradual.
- **Reset contract:** everything installed is cleared by `force_clear`/`escape`/
  `/purge` (see `FACILITY_DESIGN §7`).
- **Faction:** The Facility is a real faction; standing drives title/grade/sheet.
- **Voice:** cold institution, warm cruelty, real degradation, inevitability,
  the floor underneath. Write it filthy.
