roomzone desc futon = The |wfuton|n against the northern wall is a deep, yielding yellow, piled with |xpillows and blankets|n in warm golds and creams, the layers accumulated over time rather than arranged. It has the look of something slept in rather than displayed — a dip worn into the center, the topmost blanket always slightly displaced, a pillow that has been hugged so often it holds a shape on its own. It is the most obviously lived-in surface in the room, and it is the warmest.

roomzone desc collar_wall = Above the futon, a |wdark collar|n of thick leather with |csilver studs|n has been chained to the wall on a long, |csteel-linked leash|n, its length calculated to allow the bed and a little beyond. The collar hangs at rest now, the bell on its front catching any air that moves through the room and returning it as a faint, clear chime. The engraving on the bell reads |m'Helena's Momo'|n, the letters worn to a particular softness at the fingertip contact points that suggests they have been read many times, and not always by a standing person.

roomzone desc pillory = At the center of the room, an |woak and steel frame|n supports an adjustable |wpillory|n — the slot for neck and wrists mounted on a vertical track that allows it to be set low, level, or raised until whoever is locked in it is forced onto the balls of their feet. The wood is pale and close-grained, the steel hardware a dull, functional black that does not reflect. Below, set into an open cabinet at the base of the frame, a |mmilking machine|n sits in careful arrangement: the cups coiled on their hoses, a small row of glass bottles clean and waiting, a tin of skin lotion beside them with the lid left slightly loose.

roomzone desc tack_wall = The eastern wall holds the leather work: |wpurple and black harnesses|n arranged by size, several bridles with their buckles aligned, two coils of rope, and a |wcrop|n hanging by its loop at the far end. The leather is clean and conditioned, the metal rings and buckles free of tarnish. It is orderly in the way that things are orderly when they are used and returned rather than simply stored. The smell of leather oil carries from here across the room on still days.

roomzone desc bench_pedestal = Near the pillory, an |wadjustable bench|n sits at a height that suggests kneeling is the intended position — padded in dark vinyl, the adjustment mechanism on the side locked at a setting that has not been changed in some time. Beside it, a |wcurious pedestal|n rises to about hip height, a single |mfeather|n mounted at the top on a flexible stem, angled slightly inward. The feather is pristine, which is somehow more unsettling than if it were worn.

The vertical track allows the entire board to be positioned anywhere from floor level to a height that requires standing on tiptoe, and the tilt mechanism at the base can angle the whole frame forward, shifting weight.

roomzone detail tack_wall/bridles = Three |wbridles|n hang in a row, each in the same purple-and-black coloring as the harnesses. -- STEP 8: Handle interactions --

roomzone handle futon/blankets = You pull the top blanket back and find the layers underneath in exactly the order they have always been — the heavy knit throw closest to the mattress, the lighter ones above, and that small soft blanket tucked at the pillow. The whole pile gives under your hands like something that knows how to hold a person. The warmth that comes up from the center of the bed is not residual heat from the day. It is the specific warmth of something that has been slept in recently, and will be again.

roomzone handle collar_wall/collar = You take the collar from its hanging position and it settles into your hands with a weight that is denser than it looks — the leather is thick, the studs solid silver, nothing hollow about any of it. The inside lining is a soft suede that gives slightly under a fingertip. You turn it to the bell, and even the small motion of lifting sets it ringing — clear, light, carrying across the room. The engraving faces outward, the letters catching light: |mHelena's Momo|n. You hold it for a moment. It is clearly not a prop.

roomzone handle collar_wall/bell = You cup the bell in your palm and it stops ringing immediately, the sound cut off clean. The metal is warm. You release it and it swings on its ring, finding its note again almost at once — one clean chime, then silence, then the faint continuation of the tone in the air longer than you expect. The bell was made to be heard.

roomzone handle pillory/hardware = You set your hands on the pillory board and feel the smoothness of it — the kind that only comes from the specific friction of skin over wood, over and over, until the wood polishes itself to the shape of contact. The bolt that closes it operates with one quarter-turn, simple enough to manage with one hand. You run your thumb along the inside edge of the neck slot, and the curve is right: not too tight, not loose. Someone worked this until it fit correctly. You stand in front of it and understand very clearly what it is designed to do.

roomzone handle tack_wall/crop = The crop comes off its hook with the particular feel of a handle that has been worn to fit a grip — the leather compressed just right in the palm zone, the balance forward-heavy the way a working crop should be. You turn it over. The keeper at the tip shows the small scuff-marks of use, nothing dramatic, everything purposeful. You let it rest across your open palm and the weight is familiar in the way that functional things are familiar. It goes back on its hook without a sound.

roomzone handle bench_pedestal/bench = You kneel onto the bench and the height is right immediately — the pressure distributed across the knees correctly, the angle of the hips set forward by the slope of the padding. The vinyl is cool at first and then not. The adjustment lever is within reach but you do not touch it. Whatever height this is, it is the height that is used, and you stay at it for a moment, aware of exactly where the pillory would be in relation to you from here.

roomzone handle bench_pedestal/pedestal = You move the pedestal's feather stem slightly, tilting it, and the feather arcs through the air in a slow, deliberate curve. Even the motion of setting it back to center is enough: the tip of the feather brushes the back of your hand in passing, and the contact is so light it barely registers as touch, which is exactly the point. You step back. The feather resettles on its own, turning in the air with the patience of something that knows it will be used eventually.
