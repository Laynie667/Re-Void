-- STEP 8: Handle interactions --

roomzone handle mural/figures = You stand close to the mural and move along it slowly. At this range the brushwork is visible — the wolf's coat is built from individual strokes in several overlapping blacks, and the woman's hair was painted in long, single passes from root to end. The pillory in the composition is rendered with the meticulous attention of someone who examined the actual object before painting it: the brass wheel is there, the velvet lining is there, the cord on the drop-pin is there. You find the worn point at the woman's wrist without looking for it. The paint is smooth under your fingertip, the surface different from the rest of the mural.

roomzone handle mural/text = You crouch at the base of the mural and read — or trace, if the language is unfamiliar — the line of text along the baseboard. It runs the full width of the room without interruption. The script is small enough that reading it requires proximity, which means being close to the floor, which means the mural's figures are above you as you read what is written beneath them. Whatever the text says, the position from which it must be read was chosen deliberately.

roomzone handle bed/cuffs = You lift the nearer cuff from the headboard and hold it. The padding is dense and soft, the stitching firm, the clasp mechanism exactly as described — a squeeze to release, a press to close. You work it a few times with your free hand: it closes cleanly and opens cleanly and the mechanism is smooth from use. The chain pays out its full length from the headboard loop and then holds, approximately wrist-to-headboard distance for someone lying at the top of the bed. The rabbit watches from the other pillow.

roomzone handle pillory/stocks = You lift the stock bar and let it fall open on its hinge. The velvet lining in the cutouts is immediately evident — the neck cutout is genuinely soft, cushioned enough that the pressure of the wood would be distributed rather than concentrated. You rest your wrists in the wrist cutouts without closing the stock. The height is correct for a person to stand with their arms extended forward at a natural angle. Looking up from this position, the mural fills your entire forward view. You reach for the drop-pin cord with your free hand and understand at once why it is positioned where it is.

roomzone handle milking_machine/cups = You lift one of the connected cup assemblies and flex the rubber in your hands. It yields with gentle pressure, the seal rim maintaining its shape. The suction, when the foot pedal engages, would be consistent and adjustable — the gauge on the frame has gradations marked in increments small enough to be precise. The bench padding is softer than it looks, and the angle at which it meets the machine frame is calibrated so that a person kneeling on it is positioned correctly relative to the cup assemblies without adjustment.

roomzone handle chest/surface = You lift the bear from the lid and set it aside to open the chest. The stickers shift slightly under your hands as the hinges engage — the lid is light and balanced, opening fully and staying open. The stuffed animals in the top layer are arranged in a specific order that is disrupted when you move them. Beneath the cloth wrapping at the bottom of the chest, the other items are organized with the same care as the toys above them: nothing is buried or forgotten here.
